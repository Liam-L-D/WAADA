About Hannah Shi

American Citizen | Freelance Writer | Event Organizer
Date of Birth: October 17, 1967 | Birthplace: Ejinagi, Inner Mongolia | Current Residence: Albany, New York, U.S.A.

Education

  • 1985 – 1989: Bachelor of Arts in Chinese Language and Literature, Lanzhou University, China.

Experience

  • 1989 – Present: Worked for various newspapers and media companies in Hainan Province and Beijing, China.
  • 1997: Directed the “Candlelight Project” at World Vision China (NGO).
  • 1999: Initiated and organized China’s first “World Nursery Rhyme Day,” promoted by UNESCO.
  • 2004: Founded Beijing Xinyili Love Family Cultural Company and the “Zhongyuan Children’s Home” in Beijing.
  • 2006: Served as a Chinese teacher at Yizhi Chinese Academy in San Francisco, promoting Chinese language and culture through folklore, children’s songs, and festivals in American contexts.
  • 2013 – Present: Initiated and co-founded the Washington Arts and Design Association (WAADA), serving immigrant artists and communities across the Greater Washington D.C. area and North America.

Hannah Shi has dedicated her career to bridging cultures through language, education, and community service. Her work spans journalism, non-profit leadership, and cross-cultural arts programming.
