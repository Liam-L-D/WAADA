About Liam Duan

Liam Duan is a web and visual designer who integrates creative storytelling with front-end development to support nonprofit and community organizations. Trained in Graphic Design and Information Design & Technology (M.S., SUNY Polytechnic Institute), he focuses on building accessible, lightweight websites that communicate artistic programs clearly and effectively.

Within the Washington Arts and Design Association (WAADA), Liam has played a key role in developing unified design systems, standardizing navigation and layout structures, and creating cohesive visual frameworks for event pages, artist profiles, and gallery collections. His work emphasizes clarity, usability, and a consistent visual identity across all digital platforms.

Based in Albany, New York, Liam also leads Duan Studio, where he collaborates with cultural and educational organizations to enhance their online presence through thoughtful information design and visual communication. His work reflects a balance between aesthetics, function, and purpose — helping communities connect through art and design.

Key Contributions

  • Led the development of WAADA’s digital identity and web presence, establishing cohesive design standards and visual consistency across platforms.
  • Bridged visual design and front-end implementation, ensuring the association’s online materials are both aesthetically refined and technically robust.
  • Introduced modern information design principles that enhance communication clarity and audience engagement for arts and cultural programs.
  • Collaborated with multidisciplinary members to align creative direction, web structure, and public-facing communication strategies.
  • Advocated for accessible, sustainable, and user-centered design practices that reflect WAADA’s mission of cultural exchange and artistic collaboration.
