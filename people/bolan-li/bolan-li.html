About Bolan Li

Bolan Li, born in 1963 in Shanxi, China, is a Han Chinese artist and educator. He graduated from the Oil Painting Department of the China Academy of Art in 1987.

His artistic pursuits span multiple disciplines, including oil painting, photography, video, performance art, installation, design, and mixed media. Over the years, Bolan has participated in numerous exhibitions both in China and internationally, showcasing his diverse creative vision and commitment to artistic innovation.

In addition to his artistic practice, Bolan has built a longstanding career as a university educator, teaching painting, photography, and interior and exterior space design. He has also worked independently on various design projects — such as creating the logo for the Beijing Agricultural College — and has served as a design consultant for multiple architectural and spatial design initiatives.

Beyond his teaching and design work, Bolan is an avid collector with nearly two decades of experience, focusing particularly on Chinese historical photography. His personal archive contains approximately fifty thousand old Chinese photographs, and he has contributed to the establishment of the Chinese Portrait Yearbook, reflecting his deep passion for cultural preservation and visual history.

Key Contributions

  • Graduated from the China Academy of Art, Department of Oil Painting (1987).
  • Exhibited extensively in both domestic and international art exhibitions.
  • Educated university students in painting, photography, and space design for many years.
  • Undertook independent design projects, including institutional logo and spatial design consulting.
  • Built a private collection of ~50,000 historic Chinese photographs, contributing to the creation of the Chinese Portrait Yearbook.
