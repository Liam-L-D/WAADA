Call of Autumn

With Poetry, Painting and Music Calling for Love, Peace and Hope.

It all begins with an idea. Maybe you want to launch a business. Maybe you want to turn a hobby into something more. Or maybe you have a creative project to share with the world. Whatever it is, the way you tell your story online can make all the difference. Don’t worry about sounding professional—sound like you. There are over 1.5 billion websites out there, but your story is what’s going to separate this one from the rest.

Autumn, with its poetic allure, paints a canvas of reflection and gratitude as leaves dance in the wind. The gentle sunlight bathes the world in hues of red and gold, awakening the senses to the beauty of life's fleeting moments.

In the embrace of autumn's charm, the Trinity Cultural Exchange Center joined hands with Kunlun Publishing House, Borderless Culture & Art Center, and the Washington Art and Design Association to orchestrate the “Call of Autumn” in Maryland on October 14th. Through the mediums of poetry, painting, and music, artists convened to champion messages of love, peace, and hope.

A poignant moment unfolded as a distinguished Singaporean actor recited the short poem “Tribute to the Soldiers” in Mandarin during the cultural exchange gathering. Accompanied by visuals captured in Ukraine by the Nevada fine arts commissioner, and enriched by the English recitation of a Korean-American poet, the experience was further elevated by the melodic strains of Ukrainian folk music performed by a local guitarist.

Even in the aftermath of the event, the spirit of creativity lingered, as the poet, writer, and artist unfettered by borders found inspiration in cursive calligraphy, translating auditory art into captivating visual expressions. Truly, a treasure to behold!
